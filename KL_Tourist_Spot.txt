Location: Petronas Twin Towers (KLCC)
Description: Iconic 88-story twin skyscrapers in the heart of Kuala Lumpur. 
Attractions: The double-decker Skybridge on the 41st floor, Observation Deck on the 86th floor, and Suria KLCC shopping mall at the base.
Operating Hours: Tuesday to Sunday, 9:00 AM - 9:00 PM. Closed on Mondays.
Best time to visit: Evening to see the towers illuminated.

Location: Batu Caves
Description: A massive limestone hill featuring a series of caves and Hindu temples, located just outside the city center.
Attractions: The steep climb of 272 colorful steps, the towering golden statue of Lord Murugan, and the main Temple Cave.
Operating Hours: Daily, 7:00 AM - 9:00 PM.
Entry Fee: Free for the main temple cave.

Location: Kuala Lumpur Tower (KL Tower)
Description: A communications tower offering one of the highest viewpoints in the city.
Attractions: Indoor Observation Deck, open-air Sky Deck, and the glass-bottomed Sky Box.
Operating Hours: Daily, 9:00 AM - 10:00 PM.

Location: Dataran Merdeka (Independence Square)
Description: The historical square where the Malayan flag was first hoisted in 1957.
Attractions: Sultan Abdul Samad Building architecture, the 95-meter flagpole, and the nearby Kuala Lumpur City Gallery.

Location: Bukit Bintang
Description: The main shopping and entertainment district in Kuala Lumpur.
Attractions: Numerous shopping malls including Pavilion KL, Lot 10, and Berjaya Times Square. The area is also close to Jalan Alor Food Street, which is famous for its local dining experience.

Location: Central Market (Pasar Seni)
Description: A historic landmark built in 1888 that started as a humble wet market. 
Attractions: A bustling center offering local crafts, textiles, souvenirs, and a unique cultural immersion. It is located right next to Kasturi Walk.

Location: Perdana Botanical Gardens
Description: A peaceful 91-hectare park located in the heart of the city.
Attractions: Features a large artificial lake, the Orchid and Hibiscus Gardens, a palm garden, and a herb and spice garden.

Location: Thean Hou Temple
Description: One of the largest and oldest temples in Southeast Asia, offering a retreat from the busy city life.
Attractions: An octagonal hall with Buddhist pagodas, a Chinese herb garden, a wishing well, a turtle pond, and sculptures of the 12 Chinese zodiac signs.
